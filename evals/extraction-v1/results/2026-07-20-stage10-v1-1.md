R6 is the like-for-like result for the final v1.1 code: the v1 schema and
rolling context, a targeted admission rule for the known `I'd keep`
preference-tail leakage, a required model-supplied identity for mutable status
and handoff candidates, and v1.1 replay-safe optional-time decoding. It matched
v1 raw recall, exceeded v1 on source-supported recall, and eliminated the
`knowledge_update_27` leakage with zero provider errors.

R3 was a preliminary narrow-policy run before the final classifier isolation;
its stronger 9/23 raw result is not used as the final claim.

R5 evaluated broader non-committal-language markers plus removal of rejected
candidates from subsequent rolling Episode context. The policy was cleaner and
faster but reduced source-supported recall from the v1 gate of 5/11 to 4/11.
This is a quality no-go, so that aggressive policy was rolled back.

Two earlier attempts are not acceptance evidence. R1 and R2 still leaked the
known superseded event while the exact preference-tail rule was being repaired.
R4 used the aggressive policy but failed two cases because the provider emitted
empty strings for optional timestamps. V1.1 now normalizes those empty optional
times to null; R5 completed all 111 calls without error.

## Decision

- Keep production and blank-configuration defaults on v1.
- Keep v1.1 opt-in and experimental.
- Retain the narrow leakage repair, required identity presence, protocol-isolated
  Episodes, and the v1.1-only empty-time decoder.
- Roll back broad marker admission and filtered Episode persistence because the
  fixed source-supported gate regressed.
- Require a second valid narrow-policy cohort before considering v1.1 promotion;
  one favorable stochastic run is not enough to replace v1.

Token F1 was not used as the acceptance criterion. The decision follows leakage,
source-supported Atom recall, provider validity, and stage latency.
